Medication Activity Checklist (Assessee)

Medicines
Support plan checked
Hands washed and resources prepared prior to administration
MAR chart and associated paperwork checked to ensure person has not already taken the medicines
Medicines selected ready for administration in accordance with the MAR
Expiry date of medicine checked
Allergy/drug sensitivity checked on MAR
Label checked against MAR
6-point check carried out (RIGHT person, medicine, dose, time, route and right to refuse) plus cautionary warnings checked
Administration
Medicine taken to the person
Identity of person checked ‚Äì to ensure that medication has not been mixed up with other household members.
Dose prepared and MAR sheet dotted (paper MAR only)
Person informed their medicine is ready
Ensures person is in an upright position (if oral administration)
Medicines given according to best practice checklist* (and drink offered for oral medicines)
Observes person taken their medicines
Records and signs immediately on the MAR for what has been given or enters code.
Demonstrates or knows correct procedure for refused medication
Demonstrates or understands correct procedure for administering 'PRN' medication and using the PRN Protocol
Understands correct procedure for dealing with a medicines error and what to do if missing signatures are identified on the MAR.
Liquids
Check expiry date
Shakes the bottle.
Uses an appropriate measuring device.
If using a measuring cup, checks the amount poured at eye level on a flat surface.
Pour the bottle with the pharmacy label facing up to minimise spillage onto the label and making it difficult to read.
Is using an oral syringe, removes air gap and directs the syringe to the side of the mouth, skirting a little liquid in at a time.
Wipe around the neck of the bottle after measuring to keep the container clean and keep it easy to open for next use.
Creams
Check expiry date.
Keeps the tube with the labelled box - Marks new tubes/ or box with date of opening and the new expiry date (if applicable)
Puts on clean pair of gloves.
If applying moisturiser, applied to dry skin, applies cream down the limb in the direction of the hair growth using sweeping motions. For fragile skin, uses a cupping action with both hands
If applying a Steroid cream, administers a small amount using fingertips units (using Patient Information Leaflet)
If applying a steroid cream, applies cream thinly.
If using both a steroid and an emollient, it is important to leave sufficient time (approx.30 mins) between the two treatments ‚Äì is this feasible given time for calls
If applying a barrier cream, applies as per directions.
Monitors for any signs of skin irritation or reaction and follows correct procedure if this occurs
Shows awareness of fire risk with emollients.
Eye Drops
Check expiry date.
Mark new bottles with date of opening and the new expiry date (if applicable)
Inspect the eye drops, even if still in date
Hands washed and gloves worn, if appropriate
Ensures the client is upright
Confirm which eye the drop(s)/ointment is for
Shakes the bottle and takes the top off the bottle and puts it down somewhere clean
Tilts client‚Äôs head backwards and gently rolls down lower lid
Holds dropper above the eye and squeeze a drop inside lower eyelid
Wipes away any liquid from the client‚Äôs cheek with a clean tissue
Administer in the other eye if drops are prescribed for both eyes
If using more than one drop, waits a couple of minutes before instilling the second drop
If using gel or ointment, squeezes about 1cm into eye pouch, unless otherwise directed
Replaces the top on the bottle
Ear Drops
Check expiry date
Mark new bottles with date of opening and the new expiry date (if applicable)
Inspect the ear drops, even if still in date
Hands washed and gloves worn, if appropriate
Ensures the client is upright
Confirm which ear the drop(s)/ointment is for
Shakes the bottle and takes the top off the bottle and puts it down somewhere clean
Tilts client‚Äôs head to one side
For adults or children over three, gently pull the top of the ear upward and backward. This will straighten the ear canal.
For children under three, gently pull the bottom of the ear back and down. This will straighten the ear canal.
Instils the correct number of drop (s) into the ear squeezing the bottle gently if necessary
Keeps the head tilted for a couple of minutes to let the drops penetrate
Straightens the head and wipes away excess liquid with a clean tissue
Repeats for the other ear if required
If you are instilling drops into both ears, wait 5-10 minutes between ears to allow the ear drops to run into the ear canal
Nose Drops
Check expiry date
Mark new bottles with date of opening and the new expiry date (if applicable)
Inspect the nose drops, even if still in date
Hands washed and gloves worn, if appropriate
Ensures the client is upright
Asks the client to blow their nose gently into a tissue
Shakes the bottle and takes the top off the bottle and puts it down somewhere clean
Gently tilts clients head back
Instils required number of drops into each nostril
Asks client to keep head tilted back from 2 minutes
Replaces the top
Inhalers
Client is in an upright position
Removes cap and shakes inhaler
If inhaler is new or has not been used for a week or more, spray it into the air first to check it works.
Asks client to breathe out as much as possible then clasp mouthpiece in the mouth ensuring lips are sealed around it
Asks client to breathe in and at the same time press the top of the inhaler down to release a puff of medicine.
Asks client to hold their breath for 10 seconds
If a second puff is required, waits 30 seconds before starting again
If administering a preventer, asks client to rinse their mouth with water after using inhaler.
Fits inhaler into so that inhaler is pointing straight up
Ensures mouthpiece is clasped in the mouth
Presses one puff into the spacer
Asks client to breathe gently through the spacer for 10 breaths
If a second puff is required, waits 30 seconds before pressing another puff into the spacer. Repeat as with first puff
Knows to wash and rinse spacer and leave to dry naturally
Patches
Check expiry date
Knows date patch is due to be changed, checks dose instructions, manufacturer‚Äôs leaflet and MAR that should be clearly marked with this information
Applies patch at the same time of day on each due date
Uses Transdermal Patch Application Record to check if a previously applied patch needs to be removed.
Puts on gloves and removes patches by gently peeling away from the skin taking care not to touch the inside of the patch & folds removed patch in half with the medicated side to the middle for safe disposal.
Records date of patch removal on the Transdermal Application Record and identifies new site of application that is different to the old site. Use‚Äôs manufacturers information to know where on the body the patches should be applied.
Before applying a patch, checks skin Is clean, dry, non-greasy and non-hairy.
Opens package carefully taking care not to damage the patch
Applies patch appropriately and gently applied pressure to the entire patch using palm of hand to ensure patch sticks to the skin
Makes a record of application on the Transdermal Application Record
Demonstrates knowledge that heat affects absorption of medicines through the skin. Knows not to apply patches directly after having a bath or a shower, or if a client has a fever, and that hot water bottles/electric blankets etc should not be used if a patch is applied
Checks and records daily that prescribed patches are applied correctly and still in place.
